Product DesignerB2B SaaSDesign System
splash technologies
PROBLEM

Scaling design without a system

As the product grew, inconsistencies multiplied across features and teams. There was no shared language between design and engineering, which slowed development and created a fragmented user experience. I set out to build a design system that could scale with the company.

APPROACH

Build the foundation, then the features

I audited every screen and component in the product, cataloged patterns, and identified gaps. From there I built a token-based design system with reusable components in Figma, wrote usage guidelines, and partnered with engineering to implement it in code. The system covered color, typography, spacing, elevation, and interactive states.

IMPACT

Faster shipping, fewer inconsistencies

The design system reduced design-to-dev handoff time significantly and brought visual consistency across the entire product. It became the single source of truth for both teams and continues to evolve as the product scales.

PROBLEM

Gamers struggle to find the right teammates

Existing platforms for finding gaming partners were clunky, toxic, or buried inside larger social networks. Players wanted a dedicated space to connect based on play style, skill level, and schedule — not just the game title.

APPROACH

Design for compatibility, not just connection

I designed a mobile-first experience that profiles players by their habits and preferences, then matches them with compatible teammates. The interface needed to feel native to gaming culture while remaining welcoming to casual players. I focused on quick onboarding, smart filters, and real-time availability signals.

IMPACT

A community that keeps coming back

GameGether saw strong retention in its first months, with users returning to find new teammates regularly. The matching system reduced friction in team formation and the community tools encouraged positive interactions.

PROBLEM

Design-to-code handoff is still broken

The gap between design and production has always created friction — specs drift, components get reinterpreted, and the final product rarely matches the original intent. I wanted to challenge that assumption by building something real entirely within Figma Make, without touching a traditional codebase or handing anything off.

APPROACH

Design and ship in the same tool

TypeRace is a fully functional typing speed test, designed and built end-to-end using Figma Make. I designed the interface, wrote the logic for real-time WPM tracking and accuracy scoring, and shipped it as a live web app — all within one environment. Daily rotating challenges were added to give repeat users a reason to come back, and the visual feedback system was tuned to feel responsive without being distracting.

IMPACT

A live proof of concept

TypeRace is live and publicly accessible. More than just a side project, it's a demonstration of what modern design tooling makes possible: a designer shipping a production-quality product without a traditional engineering handoff. It reflects the kind of cross-disciplinary ownership I bring to every project.
